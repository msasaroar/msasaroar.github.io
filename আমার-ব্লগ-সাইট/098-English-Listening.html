English Listening Levels

What Are the Levels of English Listening?

  • A1 – Beginner: Understand basic words and greetings.
  • A2 – Elementary: Understand simple expressions related to everyday life.
  • B1 – Intermediate: Understand the main points of conversations and instructions.
  • B2 – Upper Intermediate: Understand more complex speech, including media content.
  • C1 – Advanced: Understand abstract topics and fast conversations.
  • C2 – Proficient: Understand nearly everything, even in challenging conditions.

How Can I Determine My Level?

  1. Take an Online Test: Try free tests on British Council, EF SET, or Cambridge English websites.
  2. Self-Assessment: Match your abilities to real-life tasks (see table below).
  3. Practice Listening by Level: Use sites like Elllo.org or BBC Learning English.
  4. YouTube Practice: Search for level-based listening videos and test with subtitles.
  5. Track Progress: Keep notes of new words and what you understood or missed.
